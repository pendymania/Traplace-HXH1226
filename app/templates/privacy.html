1. Introduction

Welcome to Kingshot Traplace ("we," "our," or "us"). We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring transparency about how we handle data. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and your rights regarding your data.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Anonymous Analytics Data

With your consent, we collect anonymous usage data through Google Analytics 4 to understand how our service is used and improve user experience. This includes:

  • Page views and visit duration
  • Referral sources (how you found our site)
  • Browser type and device information
  • General geographic location (country/city level, not precise)
  • Language preference
  • Whether you accessed via a shortened URL (pattern only, e.g., /s/*)

We do NOT collect:

  • Specific shortened URL codes
  • Your map data stored in URL hash fragments
  • Block configurations or custom labels
  • Personal identifying information
  • IP addresses (anonymized automatically)

2.2 Local Data Storage

Your map data is stored locally in your browser via:

  • URL Hash Fragments: All block positions, colors, and labels are encoded in the URL after the # symbol. This data never leaves your browser unless you share the URL.
  • Cookies: Only used to remember your analytics consent preference (traplace_analytics_consent).

2.3 Shortened URLs

When you create a shortened URL (/s/<code>), we temporarily store the mapping between the short code and your original URL path in Redis with a time-to-live (TTL). This data is:

  • Stored for 7 days from last access
  • TTL automatically renewed to 7 days when the shortened URL is accessed
  • Not linked to any personal information
  • Automatically deleted after expiry

3. How We Use Your Information

  • Analytics: To understand usage patterns, improve features, and fix bugs
  • Service Operation: To provide shortened URL functionality
  • Legal Compliance: To comply with applicable laws and regulations

4. Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share your data with third parties, except:

  • Google Analytics: Anonymous analytics data is processed by Google according to their privacy policy
  • Legal Requirements: If required by law or to protect our rights

5. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Opt-out of analytics: Click "Reject" on the cookie consent banner
  • Delete your data: Clear your browser cookies and URL history. No server-side profile exists.
  • Access your data: All your map data is visible in the URL hash fragment

6. Data Security

We implement reasonable security measures including:

  • HTTPS encryption for all connections
  • Secure cookie flags (SameSite=Lax; Secure)
  • IP address anonymization in Google Analytics
  • No persistent user profiles or accounts

7. International Users

Our service is accessible worldwide. Analytics data may be processed by Google in various countries. We anonymize data to comply with GDPR and similar privacy regulations.

8. Children's Privacy

Our service does not target children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last updated" date.

10. Contact Us

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy, please contact us via our GitHub repository.
